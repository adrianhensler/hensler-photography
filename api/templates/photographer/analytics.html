How Metrics Are Collected

All analytics are collected automatically as visitors interact with your public gallery. Data is anonymous and aggregates visitor behavior to help you understand what resonates with your audience.

What Each Metric Means

  • Impressions: Times an image became visible in the viewport (at least 50% visible while scrolling). Shows which images attract attention.
  • Clicks: Times a visitor clicked a thumbnail in the gallery grid. Indicates strong interest in viewing the full image.
  • Views: Times the full-screen lightbox was opened. Represents deep engagement with your work.
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): Percentage of impressions that resulted in clicks. Industry average: 2-5%, excellent: 10%+. Shows image appeal strength.
  • View Rate: Percentage of clicks that opened the lightbox. Usually 95-100%. Low rates may indicate technical issues.
  • Average Duration: Mean seconds spent viewing full-screen images. Typical: 2-5s quick look, 5-10s normal interest, 10+s high engagement.

Privacy & Data

What's collected: Event types, timestamps, which images were viewed, where visitors came from (referrer), and session IDs (random strings).

What's NOT collected: IP addresses, names, emails, personal information, tracking cookies, or any data that identifies individual visitors. All data is anonymous and aggregated.

Understanding Scroll Depth

Shows how far down visitors scroll through your gallery. Higher percentages at deeper depths indicate engaging content. It's normal for fewer visitors to reach 100% - attention naturally falls off as users scroll.
