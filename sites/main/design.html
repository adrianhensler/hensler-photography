hensler photography · management console direction

easy.

This page is the working design brief for the site's photographer console, published as part of building this site in the open. It is not a finished interface; it is the plan the console is being built toward.

One word is the whole spec. The console should feel like the quiet back room of the gallery: cotton-rag paper in the light, the darkroom after hours in the dark. The photographs supply all the color; the interface supplies calm. The intelligence works out of sight, and things are simply ready when you look.

Use the toggle at the top of this page to switch between the two grounds: cotton rag (light) and darkroom (dark). The serif here is Playfair Display, the same face used on the public hero.

palette

cotton rag & darkroom

Both themes are warm-neutral, built around the sand accent the public site already owns (#e8d5b5: the category links and focus rings on adrian. and liam.). On the light ground the accent deepens to bronze so it can carry buttons; on the dark ground the sand is used straight, which ties the console to the public gallery exactly.

the upload flow

things are simply ready

Drop the files and walk away from the machinery. While a photo is analyzing, its fields shimmer like the public site's hero skeleton. When it's done, the title, caption, and EXIF are just there: marked only by a small sand dot meaning "suggested; edit anything." No robot badges, no cost talk, no purple. One primary action per card.

one system

buttons, standardized

Three button voices across every console page, and no others. Serif lowercase for page titles only. Mono for EXIF only. Everything interactive focuses with a sand ring.

  • The photograph is the loudest thing on every screen.Chrome stays paper-quiet; thumbnails and heroes carry the color.
  • The intelligence is invisible.No "AI" in visitor or photographer copy. Suggestions appear as finished work, marked by the sand dot until you touch or publish them.
  • One primary action per view.Publish. Everything else steps back into quiet or ⋮.
  • Skeletons, never spinners.Same shimmer the public hero uses: the console and the site are one family.

These are living notes, not a frozen spec: the console is being brought in line with them page by page, and this document updates as decisions change. Semantic colors (published-green, warning, error) sit outside the two-accent brand rule as state, not decoration.
